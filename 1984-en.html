The Book 1984

Plot:

The story takes place in 1984, in a dystopian future where the State imposes an extremely totalitarian regime for society, through the surveillance of Big Brother, imposed by the Party (Ingsoc). Where they are watched at all times by the telescreen. The Party's phrase is “War and Peace, Freedom is Slavery, Ignorance is Strength”.

The world was divided into three great powers, Oceania (where the story takes place), Eurasia and Eastasia. They were always in conflict with each other.

Oceania is divided into four Ministries, Love, Truth, Peace and Abundance. Winston Smith is the protagonist, he worked at the Ministry of Truth falsifying documents in order to alter people's past and memories.

Winston did not support the Party's ideas and throughout the narrative he tries to fight against the oppressive system. At first, he only expressed his ideas in his diary, but when he met Julia, he began to challenge the Party's impositions.

About George Orwell:

George Orwell. “George Orwell (1903-1950)is the pseudonym of Eric Arthur Blair, English writer and journalist, known for his book “1984”, whose plot takes place in a fictional country where there is a totalitarian political regime. The main character of the book is a civil servant aware of the oppression he was experiencing.

George Orwell was born in Motihari, British India, on June 25, 1903. He was the son of a public servant in the service of the crown and his mother was the daughter of a French merchant.

George Orwell died of tuberculosis in London, England, on January 21, 1950. He was buried in the All Saints’ Chur-chyard Anglican Church, where the headstone only identifies “Eric Arthur Blair”, without mentioning his pseudonym.” Source: eBiografia.
